# Pitch

## One line
"Einstein Brain is the trust layer that decides which company knowledge becomes verified, reusable memory and skills — and refuses to answer when it doesn't know."

## 30 seconds
"Lovable already gives agents Knowledge and Skills as markdown. The open question is *trust*: which facts are verified, which are stale, who approves a new one before an agent relies on it. Einstein Brain adds that — provenance on every node, a measured **abstention** bar so agents say 'I don't know' instead of guessing, and a human-gated promote step before anything becomes durable truth. Built and proven on a real SaaS. Looking for one design partner to prove it on a real workflow."

## 2 minutes
"YC's current RFS names the gap: the blocker to AI automation isn't models anymore, it's *trustworthy company knowledge* that becomes executable skills — and they say no product nails it yet.

Lovable is already most of the way there in *form*: Skills as portable markdown is exactly right. What's missing across the market is the *trust* around it: (1) **provenance** — verified vs machine-guessed, last-checked-when; (2) **abstention** — refusing when there's no grounded fact instead of hallucinating; (3) **human-gated writeback** — a new fact lands in quarantine and a person stamps it before agents trust it.

Einstein Brain does those three today as a read layer: 709 real nodes, ~97% retrieval hit, **100% abstention on unknowns — zero made-up facts on our eval set**, provenance-weighted ranking, quarantine/promote primitives. Open markdown over git — same shape as Lovable Skills, so it slots in.

Honest status: the read+trust layer is real; safe auto-write and connectors are next; executable-skill generation is roadmap. I'm not pitching a finished product — I'm pitching the trust layer Lovable Skills could sit on, and asking for one workflow to prove it together."

## The ask
A design partnership, not a sale: one workflow, two weeks, scoped read access + ~15 real questions + a workflow owner's time. Success = fewer hallucinations, abstains on unknowns, the owner says "I'd trust this." In return I ask for honest feedback, a reference, and the right to write up the result.

## Never say
"complete company brain" · "fully autonomous" · "production-ready" · "auto-write safe" · "zero hallucination" (without "on our eval set") · "better than Lovable/Glean/Copilot/Databricks." Say "governed memory layer," "production-grade read layer; governed-write in pilot," "complementary trust layer."
